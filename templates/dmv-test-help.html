Written Test Tips

1

Study the California Driver Handbook thoroughly, focusing on road rules and signs

2

Take multiple practice tests until you consistently score 90% or higher

3

Pay special attention to questions about traffic signs, right-of-way rules, and DUI laws

Frequently Asked Questions

How many questions are on the California DMV written test?

The California DMV written test consists of 36 questions. You need to answer at least 30 correctly (83%) to pass.

How long does the behind-the-wheel test take?

The driving test typically takes about 20 minutes. During this time, the examiner will evaluate your driving skills in various traffic situations.

What documents do I need to bring to the DMV for my tests?

You'll need to bring your permit, proof of insurance for the vehicle you're testing in, registration for that vehicle, and a form of identification. If you're under 18, a parent or guardian must accompany you.

What are the most common reasons people fail the driving test?

Common reasons include not checking blind spots, improper lane changes, rolling through stop signs, driving too fast or too slow for conditions, and improper parking techniques.

How many times can I take the DMV tests if I fail?

You can take the written test up to 3 times with one application fee. For the behind-the-wheel test, you can take it up to 3 times, but you'll need to wait at least 2 weeks between attempts and pay a retest fee each time.
